<p><p>The citrus pectin market is segmented into two main types based on their degree of methoxylation: High Methoxyl Pectin (HMP) and Low Methoxyl Pectin (LMP). HMP, with a degree of methoxylation greater than 50%, is primarily used in food applications for gelling and thickening, as well as in confectionery. LMP, with a degree of methoxylation less than 50%, is utilized in low-sugar formulations, dairy products, and as a stabilizer, catering to health-conscious consumers and dietary restrictions.</p></p>
